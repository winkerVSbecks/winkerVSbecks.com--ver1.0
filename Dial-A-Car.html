Nonsensical Interfaces

(Student Work)

Studying interaction design sometimes it becomes important to question the very notion of the interface you are trying to explore. These two works attempt to do that by replacing the expected interface with something completely absurd. Creating a nonsensical interface and gauging the users' reaction to it. Doing such a thing at times proves useful in highlighting an aspect of the interface which might have been otherwise overlooked.

The first piece consists of a toy remote controlled car where the conventional remote was replaced with a rotary dialer. Numbers 1-6 control the various directions the car can move in, 7-9 and 0 stop the car.

The second is an interface to make noise/music with the classic Pong game. The entire sound portfolio can be replaced by the user.

Made with Processing and Arduino.
